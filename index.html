Welcome to the machine learning class! This is an introductory course offered by the SpeeD lab.

Artificial intelligence and machine learning were highly anticipated research advances. In the 1990s we were watching Terminator performing science fiction tasks such as object detection or face recognition; today we are daily consumers of machine learning without even realising it — our mobiles can automatically detect faces in the pictures we take, email services filter out spam from the rest of the received emails, we are being recommended new items based on our shopping history. This course aims to demystify such applications, empowering you with the ability to create similar algorithms.

Learning outcomes

By the end of the course the students should be able to:

  • Identify machine learning tasks in real-word problems.
  • Use existing tools to solve such tasks.
  • Understand the components of a learning algorithm.
  • Know the main steps of applying a machine learning algorithm in practice.
  • Understand and be able to compare different learning algorithms.

Administration

This course will consist of a series of five lectures, each followed by a practical assignment. The final project is a presentation describing a machine learning solution for a problem of interest.

Students are expected to be familiar with basic programming (Python) and mathematics (linear algebra). Take a look at the following collection of exercises; this is designed to warm you up and prepare for the course.
